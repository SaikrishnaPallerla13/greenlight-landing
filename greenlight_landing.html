Customer discovery

We asked. They were honest.

Before writing a line of code, we talked to teen drivers, parents, and high schoolers about what actually happens behind the wheel.

From teen drivers
  • Most admitted to checking their phone at least once while driving.
  • Safety apps that constantly track them feel like being babysat, not helped.
  • A reward for driving well is far more motivating than a warning for driving badly.
From parents
  • They care about their kid arriving home safe — not a play-by-play of every turn.
  • Constant location tracking creates tension at home, not trust.
  • A simple weekly check-in would be more than enough peace of mind.
What changed

Our first idea wasn't it.

We started out convinced this was a phone-blocking problem. The research said otherwise.

Block the phone, solve the problem.
→
Motivate the driver, and the phone waits.
Parents want full visibility into every drive.
→
Parents want confidence, not a play-by-play.
More features means more safety.
→
Fewer, simpler features people actually use.
What we're building next

Four features. Nothing extra.

Each one exists to answer something specific we heard in research — not to pad a feature list.

Rewards system

Earn points for smooth, focused driving. Redeem them for real perks, not just a badge.

Driving score dashboard

One clear number for how the drive went. No jargon, no guilt trips.

Automatic drive detection

Starts itself the moment the car does. Nothing to remember, nothing to forget.

Parent summaries

A weekly recap parents actually enjoy reading, not a live feed to obsess over.
